Natural Disaster Dos and Don'ts


Earthquake


Detailed information about earthquakes, safety measures, and resources.




Dos

  • Drop to your hands and knees to prevent being knocked over.
  • Cover your head and neck with your arms.
  • Stay indoors and away from windows.
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings and power lines.
  • Keep a flashlight and emergency kit nearby in case of power outages.
  • Establish a communication plan.

Don'ts

  • Do not run outside during shaking.
  • Do not use elevators.
  • Do not stand in doorways.
  • Do not stay under heavy objects that could fall.
  • Do not use matches, lighters, or any open flames due to potential gas leaks.
  • Wait for emergency personnel.

Flood

Detailed information about floods, safety measures, and resources.




Dos

  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • Follow evacuation orders from authorities.
  • Avoid driving or walking through flooded areas.
  • Turn off utilities if instructed to do so.
  • Keep a battery-powered radio or phone charged to receive emergency updates.

Don'ts

  • Do not stay in areas that are prone to flooding.
  • Do not touch electrical equipment with wet hands.
  • Do not attempt to swim through strong currents.
  • Do not ignore warnings or advisories from local officials.
  • Do not use contaminated water for drinking, washing, or preparing food.

Hurricane

Detailed information about hurricanes, safety measures, and resources.




Dos

  • Listen to weather updates and evacuation orders.
  • Secure windows and doors, and reinforce your home if possible.
  • Stock up on emergency supplies like food, water, and medications.
  • Move to a safe location away from coastal areas.
  • Unplug electrical appliances to prevent damage from power surges.

Don'ts

  • Do not go outside during the storm.
  • Do not drive through flooded roads.
  • Do not use candles or open flames during power outages.
  • Do not neglect to follow local emergency instructions.
  • Do not underestimate the storm's strength or delay your evacuation plans.

Drought

Detailed information about droughts, safety measures, and resources.




Dos

  • Conserve water by reducing usage and fixing leaks.
  • Use drought-resistant plants and landscaping.
  • Follow local water restrictions and guidelines.
  • Store water for emergency use and ensure proper sanitation.

Don'ts

  • Do not waste water on non-essential activities like washing cars.
  • Do not ignore local water conservation rules.
  • Do not overwater plants or use excessive amounts of water for irrigation.
  • Do not rely on water supplies without proper purification.
